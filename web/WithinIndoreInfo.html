1.RAJWADA PALACE

Rajwada, also known as the Holkar Palace or Old Palace, is a historical palace in Indore that was constructed by the Holkars of the Maratha empire around 2 centuries ago. An example of the architecture of the time, the palace is a 7 story structure that is placed near the Holkar Chhatris. The structure has gained the attention of tourists due to its size and age.
Timings: 07:00 AM - 07:00 PM

2.LAL BAGH PALACE

Lal Bagh Palace is one of the most admired and grand monuments of Indore city, situated on the banks of Khan River on the outskirts of Indore. This palace was built by Maharaja Shivaji Rao Holkar in 1921. The building is three-storeyed and is the place where many royal receptions were held. The Government of Madhya Pradesh is converting the palace into a cultural center and it has now been converted into a museum.
Timings: 10:00 AM - 05:00 PM

3.ANNAPURNA TEMPLE

Dedicated to Goddess Annapurna, the deity of food the temple also houses the shrines of Sivan, Hanuman and Kalabhairava.The entrance of the temple is adorned with four-life size statues of elephants, and the architectural splendour of the temple makes it a must-visit for all tourists.The Annapurna Temple has a storied past, dating back to the 9th century, and underwent reconstruction in 1959 under the guidance of Mahamandaleshwar Swami Prabanandagirimharaj. The architectural fusion of Indo-Aryan and Dravidian styles gives the temple a unique identity, reminiscent of the renowned Meenakshi Temple in Madurai.
Timings: 6:00 AM - 11:00 PM

4.KHAJRANA GANESH MANDIR

Khajrana Ganesh Temple is a Hindu temple complex, situated in Khajrana area of Indore in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh, India. The main temple in the complex is dedicated to Lord Ganesha. It was built by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of the Holkar Dynasty.This temple was built in 1735 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of the Holkar dynasty, who retrieved the idol of god Ganesha from a well where it had been hidden to keep it safe from the Mughal ruler Aurangzeb. It is said that the ancient idol in the temple was seen in a dream of a local priest, Pandit Mangal Bhatt. The temple is still managed by the Bhatt family.
Timings: 05:00 AM - 12:00 PM

6.KAANCH MANDIR

The Kanch Mandir located in Indore is completely made of glass. It was built in 1903 by famous businessman Seth Hukumchand Jain. The temple is built like a palace from the outside but the inner walls, ceiling, floor, pillars and doors of the temple are all made of glass and cut glass chandeliers. The main idols of Lord Mahavir situated in the temple are decorated with black onyx and multicolored.
Artisans were specially called from Jaipur and Iran for the construction of the temple. The stained glass and mirror panels depict various aspects of Jainism with lavishly intricate details. The temple celebrates Jain festivals with great enthusiasm and enthusiasm.
Timings: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM
View Photos

 

7.CENTRAL MUSEUM

The Central Museum which was established in October 1929 in the famous Nar-Ratna Mandir. It was established primarily to collect photographs and documents of great personalities and was later converted into a full-fledged museum. Situated near the General Post Office in Indore, it houses a wide array of Parmar sculptures, scriptures and also houses a rich collection of ancient coins and armor.
It has eight galleries showcasing subject-wise artworks. Those who want to know the roots of the culture and tradition of Indore, how the rich civilization developed here, they can all come here to know.
Timings: 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM

9.KRISHNAPURA CHHATRI

Krishnapura Chhatris, also known as Krishna Pura Chhatris, are three chhatris located in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India. The Chhatris were built in honor and memory of the Holkar rulers and their ancestors. These structures served as grand tombs and monuments where the remains of the Holkar rulers were buried. Chhatri is notable for his association with one of the most famous Holkar rulers, Queen Ahilyabai Holkar, who contributed significantly to the development of Maheshwar and its heritage. His burial chamber is one of the major structures within the complex. Over time, the Chhatris have been carefully preserved and restored, serving as a link to the glorious history of the Holkar Dynasty and the Maratha Empire.
Timings: 5:00 AM - 7:00 PM
View Photos

11.BADA GANAPATI

The Bada Ganapati Temple located in Indore is dedicated to Lord Ganesha. The name of the temple is Bada Ganapati because the temple has a huge idol of Lord Ganesha about 25 feet in height, which is the largest idol of Lord Ganesha in the world.This temple was built by Pandit Narayan Dadhich in 1875. The Ganesh idol is made of limestone, jaggery, bricks, mud and water.
It is believed that the soil used for the construction of this idol was purchased from the seven holy cities of Ayodhya, Mathura, Kanchi, Maya, Avantika, Varanasi and Dwarka.
Timings: 5:00 AM to 12:00 PM in Morning and 4:00 PM – 9:00 PM in Morning
View Photos

 

12.MAHATMA GANDHI HALL

Mahatma Gandhi Hall located in Indore is considered to be one of the most beautiful buildings. Mahatma Gandhi Hall was constructed in 1904, it was earlier called King Edward Hall, but in 1948 it was changed to Mahatma Gandhi Hall. The hall is built in the Indo-Gothic style and was designed by Charles Frederick Stevens of Bombay.
It is one of the famous landmarks of the city and is the most sought-after venue for grand public gatherings, exhibitions, fairs, etc. due to its huge capacity to accommodate around 2000 people. The complex also houses a library, a temple and a children's park.
Timings:9:00 AM to 5:00 PM
View Photos

 

13.SARAFA BAJAR

Sarafa Bazar is one of the most vibrant and throbbing markets located in Indore, Madhya Pradesh. Indore's Sarafa Bazar is a popular tourist destination due to its cuisine and nocturnal lifestyle. Situated in the middle of Sarafa Bazar, Chopati is a jewelery market and a night street food court. It bustles with activity throughout the day. It trades in jewellery, gems, jewelry and other artifacts during the day and serves as a haven for chrysophilists while it turns into a paradise for gastronomes after 8:00 in the evening.
Consisting of several kiosks and stalls featuring indigenous and western cuisines, it offers a wide variety for visitors to choose from. Indore has been acclaimed for many epic specialties like Falooda Ice Cream, Pav Bhajji and many more.
Timings: 10:00 AM – 11:00 PM

14.BIJASEN TEKRI

Bijasen Tekri Temple is located on top of a hill in the city of Indore in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India. This temple is situated on Bijasen Tekri, which is a hill or Tekri in the local language. The history of the temple is linked to the rich cultural heritage of Indore. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Bijasen, who is a form of the Hindu goddess Durga. This temple was built by Shivaji Rao Holkar in the year 1760. The temple is a religious and pilgrimage site that attracts devotees and tourists alike due to its spiritual significance and panoramic views of the city. Bijasen Tekri Temple offers a unique blend of spirituality and natural beauty, making it a favorite destination for both devotees and nature lovers.
Timings: 5:00 AM to 7:00 PM
