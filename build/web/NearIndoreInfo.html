1.PATAL PANI WATERFALL

Located in Mhow Tehsil, Patalpani Waterfall is approximately 300 feet high. A hidden gem, it is a favourite picnic spot among families with its majestic beauty.According to folklore, the pit (kund) at the bottom of the falls goes as deep as patal During rainy seasons, the water gushes through great force, and hence, it is also ideal for specific adventure activities. A peaceful atmosphere offers a beautiful option to unwind for families.
Timings: 06:00 AM - 07:00 PM
Distance from Indore Bus Stand: Approximately 40 Km.
View Photos

 

2.AHILYA FORT,MAHESHWAR

Away from the noise of the world, the Ahilya Fort in Maheshwar is ranked among the favourite picnic spots for families who are interested in spending a day right next to history and river Narmada. The town of Maheshwar is 4000-year old which takes you back to an era when queen Ahilyabai Holkar ruled the place. The Fort is situated next to the picturesque ghats or river steps which offers a great site with priests chanting mantras. The Fort has been converted into a hotel which overlooks the Ahilyeshwar Temple and the river bank. The elegant architecture of the Fort will leave you mesmerised. Boat rides are the best way to enjoy the sunrise and sunset at the banks of the river.
Timings: 07:00 AM - 07:00 PM
View Photos

 

3.TINCHHA WATERFALLS

The alluring Tincha falls are a complete entertainment package if you are planning a day out with family. A canyon as well as a gorge, the waterfalls get their name as they are located in the village Tincha. With the sun on your face, eat outdoors with a beautiful view as the fall bloom after the rainy season. The water plummeting at a height of 300 ft. also has a pond for those who would like to take a swim in it. Trekking is another activity that you should indulge in if you are a nature lover if you are planning for a family day out here.
Timings: 24 Hrs
View Photos

 

4.HATYARI KHO

Do not judge this place by its name. A delightful waterfall, at a distance of 33.4 kilometres in Dongarkheda village, offers a weekend getaway packed with adventure amidst natural beauty. During the rainy season, the waterfalls offer a great view although it is not fit for trekking as it has a deep trench. The platform near the top of the falls can be a splendid spot for setting out your picnic.

5.MOHADI FALLS

The breathtaking sight of water falling from a height is always thrilling, and Mohadi Waterfalls are just the perfect spot where you would take your family to visit for a picnic. Easily accessible, the Waterfall is located at a distance of 30 kms from Indore, it is an idyllic picnic spot if you are looking for a break from the monotony of life and want to be with your family.

6.BAMNIYA KUND

Another stunning waterfall, located approximately 37 kms from Indore, Bamniya Kund is a three-tier charming wonder of nature - a campsite, a cascade and a waterfall. Enjoy a walk through the luscious green jungle to double your appetite to savour those freshly packed sandwiches for your picnic. In pleasant weather when the grey clouds loom above, spending time in the cool stream is the picture-perfect moment at this tourist spot.

7.SITLAMATA FALLS

At a distance of 60 kms from the maddening crowd of the city of Indore, Sitlamata Falls is located near a village called Manpur. The Waterfalls make a lovely spot for a picnic with a majestic view of the water gushing at a tremendous speed where you can also take a dip in the freezing water once the monsoon season arrives. There are three natural caves in the area which is believed to be the hiding place of Holkar State Pindaris.
Timings:24 Hrs
View Photos

 

8.RALAMANDAL WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

This beautiful wildlife and bird sanctuary is an ideal picnic spot for nature lovers. Established in the year 1981 for the conservation of flora and fauna in the region, Rala Mandal is 20 kms away from the city of Indore. Inside the sanctuary, there is also a palace which was built by the Holkers during their reign. To have a good view of the elephants, deer and others among animals and eucalyptus, teak, Chandan, Babool among the trees in the forest, one can opt for a camel ride. The perfect time to plan a picnic at Rala Mandal is between October and February.
Timings:9:00 AM to 6:30 PM
View Photos

 

9.GIDIYA KOH WATERFALLS

Gidiya Khoh Waterfall is situated near Indore and is essentially a monsoon fed waterfall. Hence the most ideal time to visit the waterfall is during the rainy season to experience the gurgling rush of water in all its glory and splendour.

Enjoying the perks of a strategic location of the city, Indore has plenty of retreats with vibrant greens setting the picnic mood right. Go out on trekking with your loved ones and click frame-worthy family pictures at these places with rich scenery in the background to add colours.

11.PIPLIYAPALA REGIONAL PARK

Also known as the Indore Regional Park offers a very tranquil atmosphere with a lake surrounded by lush greenery for a perfect family picnic near Indore. 12.LOTUS LAKE

13.HANUWANTIYA

One of the best Indore picnic spots for adventure tourism in the state is Hanuwantiya which is tucked away amid a group of islands called Madhya Dweep. Situated along the bank of Indira Sagar dam brims with nature and offers a range of watersports.It is a hotspot during ?Jal Mahotsav,? which magnetizes hordes of tourists every year. In fact, the annual and the largest water carnival ?Jal Mahotsav? is organized by Madhya Pradesh tourism at Hanuwantiya.
Timings:8:00 AM to 8:00 PM
Distance from Indore Bus Stand: Around 135 Km.

14.SAILANI TAPU

Overlooking the backwaters of Indira Dam, Sailani Island is another largest base for trying out the exciting water activities in Madhya Pradesh after Hanuwantiya.Located in the Khandwa district near the Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga shrine, Sailani group of islands appears as a vast spell of green charm encompassing various electrifying recreational sports like activities such as speed boating, surfing, paddle boating and cruising.

15.CHORAL DAM

Choral Dam has developed as one of the ideal picnic spots near Indore in the last couple of years. Being a dam, its original purpose was to provide water for drinking and irrigation in the region but the panoramic verdant landscape blessed with one of the tributaries of the much celebrated Chambal River has become people?s favorite.People often escape the city heat to drive to this dam where one can enjoy all sorts of boating. You can also enjoy a short hike to the waterfall.
Distance from Indore Bus Stand: Around 50 Km.
View Photos

 

16.OMKARESHWAR

Out of the 12 Jyotirlingas in the world, Omkareshwar near Indore is one among those revered sites where devotees flock from all over India to worship. The grand Sabha Mandap of the temple is erected by 60 humongous stone pillars of 14 feet height. The pillars have broad rings on each end and intricately carved forehead meditating figures. The five-storeyed temple has different deities on each floor and the river Narmada flows right next to the temple where pilgrims take bath and then enter the temple for praying with coconut and flowers.
Distance from Indore Bus Stand: Around 80 Km.
View Photos

 

17.KALAKUND

Kalakand is a lesser-known getaway which is also one of the offbeat picnic spots near Indore. After you are awestruck at the Patalpani waterfall, people often decide to not go further as there is no proper road that leads to Kalakand.Nestled in the beautiful Vindhyachal mountain valley, a train journey to this quaint hill station is an adventure worth the effort. As the train chugs up the hill blowing its whistle, you will be stunned by the vistas throughout your journey.
Distance from Indore Bus Stand: Approximately 42 Km.
View Photos

 

18.JANAPAV

Famous as Janapav Kuti, Janapav is a hill tucked away along the Mumbai-Indore highway at an elevation of 2890 feet above sea level. The forest-laden hill is the second highest peak in the Malwa region providing ample trekking opportunities to the adventure enthusiasts.The place welcomes flocks of tourists during the fair after Diwali which is held on the day of Kartik Purnima, a full moon night. Out of the many Indore picnic spots, Janapav Kuti attracts not just the tourists but trekkers, wildlife enthusiasts, and bird watchers.
Distance from Indore Bus Stand: Approximately 45 Km.
View Photos

 

19.UJJAIN

Home to the powerful and revered Mahakaleshwar Temple, Ujjain is steeped in spirituality and undoubtedly one of the top choices for places to visit near Indore. Being one of the 12 jyotirlingas and counted amongst the 7 holiest sites of Hinduism, the town draws thousands of devotees every month. Ujjain also holds the world?s largest religious gathering, the Kumbh Mela, every 12 years wherein a 100-million pilgrims throng the ghats for a holy dip in the Shipra river. Other important shrines include the Kal Bhairav temple, Mangalnath temple, and Harsiddhi temple.
Distance from Indore: 52 Km
View Photos

 

20.SIRPUR LAKE

For bird photography enthusiasts, a visit to the Sirpur Lake is mandatory. Declared as an IBA (an Important Bird Area), the lake receives around 150 different species of migratory as well as resident birds. To name a few, Eurasian Wigeon, Brahminy Shelduck, Zitting Cisticola, Open Billed Stork, Purple Heron, Short-Eared Owl, Singing Bushlark, Greater Flamingo and Bluethroat are some of the birds that can be spotted here. A butterfly park is also in the pipeline as a part of an ambitious plan for the Sirpur Lake. The Lake is spread over a whopping 800 acres. Escape the daily hustle and bustle of the mundane life to this peaceful green retreat with family and spend a day in nature's company.
Timings: 06:00 AM - 07:00 PM
View Photos

 

21.GOMATGIRI

A pilgrimage site for the Jains, Gomatgiri is located at a distance of merely 10-minutes drive from the Indore airport. A 21 ft statue of Gomateshwar which is a replica of the gigantic statue of Bahubali at Shravanabelagola is a major attraction. The statue of Gomateshwar is situated at a hill. Apart from this, there are also 24 temples, each representing the 24 Tirthankaras of Jainism. Gomatgiri is a picnic spot that will reward you with the best views of the area.
Timings: 07:00 AM - 10:00 PM
View Photos

23.JOGI BHADAK

At a distance of 32 km from Maheshwar Bus Stand, and 64 km from Indore Junction, Jogi Bhadak Waterfall is a mesmerizing waterfall situated at Dhal near Indore, Madhya Pradesh. Located on the Indore-Mumbai Highway, it is one of the picturesque waterfalls near Indore, and among the must-visit places as part of the Maheshwar Trip.Jogi Bhadak Waterfall is falling from a height of about 400 feet and is a sight to behold. The base of the falls has many streams that together form a large pool amidst the beautiful green valley. This place is perfect for hangout with friends and family where one can enjoy the amazing views of waterfalls and can relax for hours in the river water. Besides, the waterfalls also provide various trekking and hiking opportunities for adventure enthusiasts.
